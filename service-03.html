1. FDA Processes & Procedures

BACK OFFICE PROCESSES AND PROCEDURES

Purpose To provide a laid down set of processes and procedures to guide the operations of existing and new Back Officers. Policy Statement Having a defined way of doing things enhances efficiency. It is important for both existing and new staff of the Back Office to be guided in the way they undertake the duties of the Unit. This manual seeks to outline the various processes and procedures involved in the duties of the Back Office. Eligibility Criteria

• All Back Office staff.
Redemption of investments

1. Calls shall be placed to clients who have upcoming maturities. These maturity responses shall be collated after they have been received from the various Investment Officers (The Maturities Data shall then be updated). Approval or authorization letters, mails or text messages must be received from our clients particularly in the following instances:
• When the request for the redemption is coming from a Corporate Body
• When the request for the payment of the redemption is for a person other than the client for whom the investment was established. For eg. if there is a request to make redemption payment to Mabruuk when the investment has been established in the name of Irene, a letter should emanate from Irene authorizing Dalex to make payment to Mabruuk.
2. In the event that the client wants to redeem, he or she shall indicate
• The amount he or she wishes to redeem
• In whose name the payment should be made and the mode of payment which shall either be cash, cheque or a bank transfer.
3. The Samaritan software shall then be checked to confirm the availability or otherwise of the amount been requested.
4. Upon confirmation, the investment in question is retired and the indicated instruction executed. The instruction could be for a full redemption, redemption of interest or a partial redemption of investment. Where redemption is requested before maturity, the following actions shall apply:
• A discount of 25% shall be charged against the interest accrued if the investment is less than 50% of the originally established tenor.
• A discount of 20% shall be charged against the interest accrued if the investment has earned a tenor between 49% and 75% of the originally established tenor. November, 2015
• No discount shall be charged for investments which have earned a tenor of 70% or more of the originally established tenor.
5. A letter for the redemption and a statement of account are prepared and submitted to the Finance Department. The Officer in charge then prepares the associated payment instructions and seek approval from the authorized signatories.
6. If a cheque is used for the payment, the cheque is picked up by the Back Office for delivery to the client. Where a bank transfer is used, a SWIFT advice for the payment shall be taken from the Finance Unit and forwarded to the client. In any of these instances, the Back Office shall file a copy of the advice.
7. Where cheques are issued to the client, he or she shall be made to sign the Redemption Receipt Book to acknowledge receipt
8. A payment instruction which remains undelivered to a client for more than a day shall be communicated to the Relationship Manager of the client. This shall be after the Back Officer has contacted the client.
9. The Relationship Manager shall advise on the next line of action. The payment instruction could be delivered by the RO in person to the client or further calls shall be placed until the instruction is paid, delivered or collected by the client.
10. The Back Office shall ensure filing of copies of all payment instructions
11. Targeted TAT for this process shall be 24 hours.

Rollovers Where a client indicates his or her willingness to roll over the matured investment, it shall be done and an updated statement and certificate shall be sent to the client.

November, 2015 Fixed Deposit Establishment

1. All new clients shall complete the KYC form once they agree to purchase the deposit.

2. Rate and investment tenor are agreed with client by RO.
3. A receipt shall be issued to the client when payment (cash, cheque or bank transfer) is received.
If payment is by cheque, it is banked; if it is by cash, it is made available to the Finance Unit and if it is by transfer or deposit into a Dalex Account, a copy of the deposit slip shall be made available to the Back Office.
4. The completed KYC form shall be made available to the Back Office. The clients’ database shall be updated with the new client details in the Samaritan and the Excel sheet kept by the Unit.
5. A carbon copy of the receipt issued shall be made available to the Back Office Unit. The payment shall be confirmed with the Finance Department. The deposit shall be established in the Samaritan.
6. All investments established shall be authorised by the Head of Finance or an authorized officer. The Back Office copy of the receipt is then endorsed by the Head of Finance to confirm the authorization of the investment established.
7. Unless a client requests for a hard copy, all certificates for established investments shall be sent by email to the client.
8. Regular top up of investments are allowed. In the case of a top up, accrued interest shall be calculated on the existing principal (investment) and added to get a new principal. The top up shall then be added to the new principal afore stated to get a new balance. Depending on the client’s request, the original maturity date can be varied or maintained.
9. Clients who have more than one investment can request for the investments to be merged. To do this, interest shall be computed on the existing investments up to the date of the “merger” and the new balances put together as one investment.

N.B: Clients shall be required to fill the BOG AML form for all cash received at the Head Office or by a Relationship Manager that are above GH¢20,000.00.
